About Me

My name is Nicholas, and welcome to my New Media Certificate journey! I am a graphic designer who also loves cats, boba tea, and watching Adventure Time.

When trying to pinpoint the best fit for me and design and the path I wanted to pursue in my career, I discovered my fascination with human-centered design and digital media. I felt stoked to take the User Experience Strategy course! This class for me was very much a start in my UX journey. I had no idea where to start with an app design, let alone the extent of a user journey. Combine that with all of the information included in a short-term summer course? oh my! I was overwhelmed, but still highly intrigued by what Don Norman had to say and all of the psychology and theory behind UX design. While I am still new to UX design and learning everyday, I would not have been able to work at my current job with the UGA Mobile App without this experience. (More of my work to come later with the app; stay tuned!)

Intro to New Media was an eye-opening experience for me. So much of it was information overload, but at the same time that information was extremely interesting. The fact that we use computers and networks and devices in our everyday lives and had no idea how they connected (anyone else? or just me?) was mind-blowing! This class sparked an interset in me of just computers themselves. Along with that I gained a deeper understanding of how these systems and devices work, which helps me everyday in my interactions at work and home.

New Media Production brought back a sense of nostaliga for me. That feeling of being back in middle school on my grandmother's old, chunky HP laptop; using HTML and CSS to decorate my profile page on my favorites online games and forums. Sitting down in the NMI computer lab with the gorgeous view of campus, I was excited to apply my rediscovered sense of child-like curiousity when building code and seeing the changes on my website. This was only the beginning of my relationship with code and websites; I yearn to continue this feeling in my future career working with computers and graphics.

Without further ado, please take a look at the work I have created so far!
